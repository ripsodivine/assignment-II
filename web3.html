Web 3.0

Web 3.0 is an evolving concept (not fully realized) that combines two major ideas:

  • Semantic Web: using ontologies and AI to enable machines to understand the meaning of data. Data is linked not just by hyperlinks but by relationships (e.g., “London is the capital of England”). This allows intelligent agents to automatically integrate information.
  • Decentralization:using peer‑to‑peer technologies (Ethereum, IPFS) to give users ownership of their data, identity, and digital assets. No central authority (e.g., Google, Facebook) controls the platform. Cryptocurrencies, NFTs, and smart contracts are parts of this.

Web 3.0 aims to create a more intelligent, user‑centric, and decentralized web. It promises enhanced privacy, security, and control for users, while enabling new applications like decentralized finance (DeFi), metaverse, and AI‑powered services.
